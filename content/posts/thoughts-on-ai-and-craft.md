## The Question

Can AI write? Not "generate text" — it can certainly do that. But can it *write*, in the sense that a writer writes — with intention, with voice, with something to say?

I have been thinking about this a lot lately.

## What AI Does Well

AI is excellent at:
- Summarizing existing information
- Producing grammatically correct sentences
- Filling in templates
- Generating variations on a theme

These are all useful. I use AI tools for these tasks regularly, and they save me real time.

## What AI Does Not Do Well

But AI struggles with:
- Having an *opinion* that it arrived at through lived experience
- Knowing what to *leave out* — the art of omission
- Developing a *voice* that is recognizably its own
- Caring about the reader as a person, not a token consumer

These are the things that make writing *writing*, not just text generation.

## The Craft of Omission

The hardest part of writing is not deciding what to say. It is deciding what *not* to say.

> I have made this letter longer than usual because I didn't have time to make it shorter.

Every paragraph you cut makes the remaining paragraphs stronger. Every sentence you trim makes the remaining sentences sharper. AI is very good at *adding* text. It is very bad at *subtracting* it.

## Voice

A writer's voice is the sum of all their small choices: which words to use, where to break a paragraph, when to use a dash and when to use a comma. These choices are not random — they reflect a *sensibility*, a way of seeing the world.

AI can mimic voice, but it cannot *have* one. It can analyze a writer's style and reproduce its surface features. But it cannot develop the underlying taste that produced that style in the first place.

## So What?

I am not anti-AI. I use it daily. But I think we need to be clear-eyed about what it is and is not.

AI is a tool for producing text. Writing is a craft for communicating meaning. These overlap, but they are not the same thing.

The danger is not that AI will replace writers. The danger is that we will confuse text generation with writing, and forget that the latter requires something the former does not: **a person who cares**.
